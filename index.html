Elephants

Facts about elephants

  • Elephants can live to be over 70 years old.
  • Elephants are the only mammals that cannot jump.
  • The average weight for an elephant heart is about 27 to 46 pounds!
  • Elephants have the longest pregnancy of all the animals.
  • Elephants purr as a form of communication, similar to cats.
  • Elephans use their feet to listen and sense for danger.

Taking care of elephants

At the Saint Louis Zoo, elephants are trained using a system called operant conditioning

Saving the elephants

Click here to see places that save elephants.

Today, African elephants are slaughtered for their ivory in order to meet the growing demand for illegal ivory. This has led to a surge in poaching, especially in East Asia. Tens of thousands of elephants are killed each year.

A single calf is born after a gestation period of 22 months (the longest of any mammal). These calves stay with their mothers for years and are also cared for by other females in the group. Twins are extremely uncommon.
