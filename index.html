Elephants

Facts about elephants

  • Elephants can live to be over 70 years old.
  • Elephants are the only mammals that cannot jump.
  • The average weight for an elephant heart is about 27 to 46 pounds!

Saving the elephants

Click here to see places that save elephants.

Today, African elephants are being slaughtered to meet a growing demand for illegal ivory, particularly from Asia, which has led to a surge in poaching. Tens of thousands of elephants are killed each year by wildlife criminals for their ivory.

A single calf is born to a female once every 4-5 years and after a gestation period of 22 months (the longest of any mammal). These calves stay with their mothers for years and are also cared for by other females in the group.
